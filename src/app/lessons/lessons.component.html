Lesson 1:

What is Campus Journalism

Campus Journalism is an activity of collecting, writing and editing stories inside a school or campus. It enables student journalists to be a voice of the student body. In this lesson, students will gain knowledge on the concept of campus journalism as well as its importance.

Lesson 2:

Types of Campus Journalism (Part 1)

This lesson will discuss the different types of journalism such as news writing, feature writing, sports writing, and sci-tech writing. It is important for student journalists to be aware of these so that they will be knowledgeable on how should they deliver a story.

Lesson 3:

Types of Campus Journalism (Part 2)

Following the previous lesson, more types of journalism including the editorial writing, editorial cartooning, radio broadcasting, and photojournalism will be discussed in this module.

Lesson 4:

Rules in Writing (Part 1)

This lesson aims to improve the grammar and vocabulary skills of students. Grammar and vocabulary are both significant in writing. Having a mastery of these can help students, particularly student writers and journalists, to craft meaningful content.

Lesson 5:

Rules in Writing (Part 2)

The goal of this lesson is to enlighten students on the proper use of punctuation marks. Punctuation is necessary in writing because it guides the readers to understand the message of written content.
