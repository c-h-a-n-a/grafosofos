Grammar in Journalistic Writing

Grammar is a very important element of writing. Having a good grammar enables a writer to convey his/her message clearly and effectively. Good grammar is significant in writing a--- because it helps a writer to communicate professionally, as well as it adds authority to one's work.

What are the different rules of Grammar in News Writing?

• Be mindful of tenses to use

A news article is composed of events that already happened or occurred, so the use of past tense is a must. However, this tense must be avoided when writing the headline of a news.

Example:

Ms. Alice Guo, the mayor of Bamban, was invited in a senate hearing.

Example in Headline:

Alice Guo present at senate hearing

• Present tense (verb + es/s) – currently happening

Examples:

She walks down the alley everyday.

Toni goes to sleep every morning because her job is in night shift.

• Past tense (ed/d) – occurred in the past

Example: Kath visited Singapore last month.

• Future Tense (will+base verb or am/ is/ are going to + base verb) – Will happen in the future

Examples:

I will bring foods tomorrow.

We are going to Disneyland next week.

• Present Perfect (has/have + past participle) already happened with no specified time

Example: I have written a poem before.

• Past Perfect (had + past participle) a past action before another past action.

Example: I passed the semester because I had submitted all of my requirements.

• Future Perfect (am/ is/ are + to have + past participle) or (will have + past participle)

Examples:

I am going to have finished my report before the next meeting.

She will have written the script before the taping starts.

• Present Progressive (am/ is/ are + present participle) happening and continuing

Example: Ms. Karen Davila is throwing questions to Mayor Alice Guo.

• Past Progressive (was/ were + present participle) happening but did not continue

Example: She was watching a Korean series when her mom ringed her phone.

• Future Progressive (is/ are/ am + going to be +present participle) or (will be + present participle) will happen in a certain time

Examples:

She will be taking a medical course in college.

She is going to be teaching in UP starting on Monday.

• Present Perfect Progressive (has/ have/ +been + present participle) happened in the past and is continuing

Example: She has been playing piano since she was a child.

NOTE: Have is used for first person (I), second person (You) and third person plural pronouns (We, They). Has is used for third person singular pronouns (he, she, it)

• Past Perfect Progressive (had + been + present participle) an action started in the past and continued until another event in the past

Example: She had been complaining her headache for days until last week

• Future Perfect Progressive (is/ are/ am + going to have been + present participle) or (will have been + present participle)

Example: He will have been making lots of money by the time his business becomes successful.

• Use of nouns.

A noun is defined as a name of place, things, person or idea. This noun should begin with a small letter except when used as a first word in a sentence.

There are many types of nouns:

• Common noun – is a type of noun that refers to a general object or idea

Examples: animal, doctor, author, country, politician

• Proper noun – refers to a specific person, place or things. This noun should start with capital letter.

Examples: Chihuahua, Dr. Vicky Belo, Coleen Hoover, Philippines, Sen. Risa Hontiveros

• Concrete nouns – are nouns that physically exists and can interact with the senses.

Examples: philosophers, stage, doorknob

• Abstract nouns – are the ideas, thoughts, or concepts that cannot interact with the senses.

Examples: love, passion, joy, happiness

• Collective Nouns – are nouns that refer to a group or collective of animals, people or things. This type of noun should not be capitalized.

Examples:

  • faculty – group of individual teachers
  • bouquet – group of flowers with decorative wrapping
  • team – group of players
  • family – group of one or more parents and their children
  • crowd – a large number of people gathered together

Remember that collective nouns take singular verb when the action is done together and it take plural verb when the individuals composing the group or collective are emphasized.

Example of Collective noun with singular verb:

The faculty is preparing for the upcoming foundation day.

Example of collective noun with plural verb:

The faculty are in disagreement of shirt color they will wear for the upcoming foundation day. (Each member or individual in the faculty disagrees with one another.)

• Singularity and Plurality

• A singular noun takes a singular verb.

Example: Ms. Lea Santos was a speaker of the leadership seminar on May 4-5, 2024.

• Nouns connected by and often take plural verb.

Example: Karen and April were declared as dean’s listers.

• Nouns referring to the same subject takes singular verb.

Example: The artist and entrepreneur is known for his humility and good leadership.

Note: Alfred is an artist and entrepreneur.

• Do not just add –s or –es to a noun. Some nouns have the same plural forms.

Examples: crossroads/crossroads, aircraft/aircraft

Errors to Avoid in Writing a Newspaper

• Avoid FREQUENT use of pronouns.

Pronouns can be helpful. However, antecedent is a must for a sentence to be more meaningful.

• Avoid misuse of infinitives.

- to + verb is the format of an infinitive,

Example: to walk, to write, or to dance.

Do not separate to and the verb.

- Be careful of bare infinitives. Some verbs do not require to.

Example:

Wrong: Maria made him to say sorry

Right: Maria made him say sorry.

- Use bare infinitives after modal verbs.

Example:

Wrong: You must to go now.

Right: You must go now.

• Avoid confusions on articles.

Articles identify a noun to be specific or unspecific.

the

- can be used with singular, plural or uncountable nouns.

- refers to a more specific noun.

Example:

Mary and Celine were able to attend the party of their teacher.

(Here, it is specified that they are going to the party of their teacher)

- a/an is an indefinite article.

- less specific.

- a is used when it precedes a word that begins with a consonant letter, and an is used when the succeeding word begins with a vowel.

Example:

Please give me a pen; any color will do.

(Here, there's no specific type or brand of pen indicated.)

• Avoid confusions between "to" and "for" as prepositions.

- The reason for to is always in verb form.

Example:

She is here to communicate with you.

(Communicate is a verb)

- "for" is always followed by a noun.

Example:

Example: She is here for communication.

(Communication is a noun)

• Avoid contractions.

Newspaper writing is formal.

doesn't - does not

don't - do not

should've - should have

• Avoid errors in capitalization.

The first letter of proper nouns are capitalized, while a small letter is a must with common nouns.

Vocabulary in Journalistic Writing

Vocabulary refers to a group of words known by a person in a language Merriam Webster’s dictionary defines vocabulary as “stock of words used in a language, by a group or individual, or in relation to a subject.”

Importance of Wide vocabulary in Journalistic Writing

  • 1. Use of wrong words will affect the whole meaning of an article. Right words must be used in describing or reporting an event.
  • 2. Words will shape the thoughts and emotions of the readers; therefore, a writer or journalist must choose words carefully.
  • 3. Limited vocabulary means limits expression of the message. With small knowledge on words, writers may find it difficult to tell the story of an event or to express his/her opinion.

How to attain a wide vocabulary?

  • 1. Read. The best way to improve vocabulary is through reading ng books, novels, dictionary, and newspaper. Through reading, you will encounter new words that will add up to your vocabulary.
  • 2. Take down notes. After you have encountered a new word, take note of its meaning. This way, you will remember it better.
  • 3. Write often. Writing is a good way of improving your vocabulary.
  • 4. Learn from context. This helps when or how to use the word appropriately. By looking for the context in a sentence, it will help you guess the meaning of a word.
  • 5. Use Language apps. With the emergence of technology, there are various applications that were developed to help you improve your vocabulary, e.g., WordUp, Duolingo,Wordscape, Memrise, etc.
