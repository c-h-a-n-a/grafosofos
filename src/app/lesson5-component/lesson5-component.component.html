Punctuation in Journalistic Writing

Punctuation marks are helpful for readers to understand a certain text or article. The absence of proper usage of punctuation marks can lead to confusion and misinterpretation.

The following are the most commonly used punctuation marks:

I. Period, comma and colon

• Period (.)

– Above all the punctuation marks, this the most commonly used. It used to end a sentence.

• Comma (,)

– this punctuation indicates a pause.

Comma is used in:

• Separating series of words and clauses of a compound and compound – complex sentence.

Examples:

Erica, Joanna, Nash, Kenneth, Coleen, Sariel, and Quincy will graduate next year.

Ms. Tolentino is a great teacher, but her students are scared by her strictness.

• Complimentary close and salutation of a letter

Examples:

Respectfully yours,

Dear Ma’am/Sir,

• Transitional and introductory phrases

Examples: She is good at language subjects. However, she failed her math subject. (Transitional)

When I was in grade school, I used to love arts and crafts. (Introductory)

• Separating an appositive

Example: Miriam Defensor Santiago, a former politician in the Philippines, was the best senator.

• Person’s position or title.

Examples: Karen Joyce Tolidanes, LPT

April Vistal, President, Researchers’ Hub Philippines

• Colon (:)

– It is used in:

• Enumerating words.

Example: The senate hearing was attended by the following senators: Sen. Risa Hontiveros, Sen. Loren Legarda, Sen. Win Gatchalian, and Sen. Jinggoy Estrada.

• Introducing a quote

Example: Dr. Jose Rizal once said: “Youth is the hope of nation.”

II. Semicolon, Dash, and Hyphen

• Semicolon (;)

Semicolon is used to :

• Join two related independent clauses in place of a coordinating conjunction.

Example: Kathlyn was studying all night; she passed the exam on the next day.

• Separate clauses or phrases that already have commas.

Example: Felly watched various movies during summer break which includes Hello love, Goodbye; The Good, the Bad and the Ugly; and Monsters, Inc.

• Dash (--)

Dash is used in:

• Place of comma to separate clauses.

Example: Bestlink College of the Philippines was able to successfully conduct its research festival – which has 16 participants – on May 2023.

• Numbers and Letters omission

Example: On June 28, 30 – Cheena celebrated her birthday.

• Introducing an enumeration

Example: She mentioned her classmates whom she caught cheating – namely: Ryle Santos, Lyza Castro, and Jhonmark De Leon.

• Hyphen (-)

Hyphen is shorter than a dash. It is used in :

• Separating compound words.

Examples: mother-in-law, editor-in-chief, fast-paced

• Connecting modifiers before a noun

Examples: well-written, short-term

• Spelled out fractions and compound numbers from twenty-one to ninety-nine.

Examples: One-half , two-thirds, seventy-six, forty-five
