Editorial Writing

- The ‘soul of the paper.’ It is an article that presents the newspaper’s opinion on an issue. It reflects the majority vote of the editorial board, the governing body of the newspaper made up of editors and business managers. In short, an editorial is an opinionated news story.

• Types of Editorial Writing

The different types of an editorial article are classified based on the purpose they serve. It can be any or a combination of the following:

Editorial of Explanation

- It helps the readers understand how the editors of a newspaper cover a sensitive or controversial issue.

Editorial of Criticism

- The Immediate purpose of these editorial is to let the reader see the problem, not the solution. However, while constructively criticizing specific actions or cases, solutions are provided as well.

Editorial of Persuasion

- Contrary to criticizing, this types of editorial aims to immediately see the solution in an issue not the problem itself. The readers will also be encouraged to take specific positive action. Political endorsements are good examples of editorials of persuasion.

Editorial of Praise

- These type of editorials commend people and organizations for something done well. Among the four this is the most uncommon type to find.

Editorial of Entertainment

- It acts as a commentary that offers a unique and informed perspective on various aspects of the entertainment world.

• Parts of Editorial

The introduction it is expected to have at least 2 paragraphs the first paragraph must contain a description of the chosen issue

  • • The first Paragraph, it can also give the background of the topic by showing facts, statistics, or figures, or anything that may give an objective interpretation of the topic.
  • • In Second paragraph, of the introduction, must present reasons why the readers must be informed about the issue.

The body has no limit in the number of paragraphs a writer may include but, there should be at least three:

  • • The First paragraph, objectively present arguments of the opposing side, the details must be complete and unbiased this help the writer establish that his point of view is better through reputation.
  • • In second, third and succeeding paragraphs, must present the writer’s arguments supporting his or her thesis statement or standard. Each argument should support by an evidence sufficient to prove the point.

The third and last paragraph is conclusion it contains two parts which is the realistic solutions and a concluding punch. The last paragraph should contain an impactful end and it should reflect the thesis statement.

• Steps to Write Editorial Writing

1. Choose the right topic.

The topic must:

  • • recent and complex
  • • affects a great number of people
  • • controversial and debatable
  • • has at least two opposing viewpoints

2. Collect information.

  • • Gather as much information as possible
  • • Do an objective report
  • • Know the opposing views
  • • Quote the actual details from sources

3. Write a thesis statement

- It helps the writer focused on the big idea he needs to address in his article.

4. Explain the issue objectively.

5. Give opposing viewpoint first with its quotations and facts.

6. Refute the other side and develop your case using facts, details, figures, and quotations.

7. Concede a point of the opposition

8. Repeat key phrases to reinforce an idea into the reader’s mind.

9. Give realistic solution to the problem that goes beyond common knowledge.

10. Wrap it up in a concluding punch that restates your opening remark.

• Structure of Editorial Writing

I. Lead with an Objective Explanation of the Issue:

  • • Include the five W’s and the H.
  • • Pull in facts and quotations from the sources which are relevant.
  • • Additional research may be necessary.

II. Present your Opposition First:

  • • Identify the people who oppose you.
  • • Use facts and quotations to state objectively their opinions.
  • • Give a strong position of the opposition.

III. Directly Refute the Opposition’s Belief:

  • • Pull in other facts and quotations from people who support your position.
  • • Acknowledge the valid point of the opposition. It will make you appear rational.

IV. Give Reasons:

  • • In defense of your position, give reasons from strong to strongest order.
  • • Use a literary or cultural allusion that lends to your credibility and perceived intelligence.

V. Conclude with Some Punch:

  • • Give solutions to the problem.
  • • A quotation can be effective, especially if from a respected source.
  • • A rhetorical question can be an effective concluder as well.

Editorial Cartoon

- It is an illustration containing a commentary that usually relates to current events or personalities. It is a simple graphic presentation of opinion that points out the problem.

• Elements of Editorial Cartoons

  • • Title
  • • Captions & Labels
  • • Symbols
  • • Caricatures/Hyperbole
  • • Stereotyping
  • • Literary/Historical Reference
  • • Point of View
  • • Main Idea

• Steps in Making Editorial Cartoons

  • 1. Know the main issue that you wish to draw a cartoon
  • 2. Illustrate through the aid of effective symbols that fit the general opinion of the editorial staff on the issue.

• Tips in Editorial Cartoon

  • ✓ Use black ink pen or pencil on white paper.
  • ✓ Employ single stroke or line.
  • ✓ Shading can be employed for emphasis.
  • ✓ Focus on one topic only.
  • ✓ Limit the use of words and labels.
  • ✓ Use universal or common symbols
  • ✓ Details in the drawing are not necessary
  • ✓ Keep it simple but the message should be clear and intact.
  • ✓ When you are drawing person, draw the facial expression and their prominent identity clearly.
  • ✓ You may apply allusion as a background scene.
  • ✓ Present the issue accurately.
  • ✓ Illustrate the idea using three to five things only.

- It is a news that is carried on a radio, television, and the internet. Some universities or colleges campus operates a radio station to provide news, information, and entertainment to the school community.

  • ✓ Downloaded Application for Music (Virtual DJ)
  • ✓ Laptop, Printer, Bondpaper, Stapler

3. Clean directional policies

4. Script

A radio script is a written material, which indicates verbal and non-verbal action that has to go into the program. It tells us what to do and say and when or how. This ensures technical accuracy and the continuity of program, and to maximize airtime.

A Radio Script Outline should have the following:

  • ✓ Opening Spiel
    • ►Time Check
    • ►Station I.D.
    • ►Station Plug
  • ✓ Intro
  • ✓ Headlines
  • ✓ Infomercial
  • ✓ Details
  • ✓ Closing Spiel

• Tips on Writing Scripts for your Listeners

  • Double or Triple space all the lines and paragraphs.
  • Use big letter/font especially those spoken lines so the news reader can easily read it.
  • All instructions and non-spoken lines should be typed in capital letters.
  • Make your script clean, short, and simple.
  • Write a page number in your script.
  • Don not use unfamiliar words.
  • Attribute the source of your news either at the beginning or at the end of the news.
  • Avoid using “this, that, the former, the latter”
  • Use active voice
  • Round-off figures/numbers

• Basic Steps in Radio Script Writing

  • Get a good briefing/know your listener
  • Do research about the topic
  • Prepare content outline
  • Write the first draft.
  • Read it aloud. Time the material
  • Visualize the script
  • Review the Script
  • Revise for style, correct timing, and accuracy

Photojournalism

- It is the communication of news through a series of photographs. This is the documentation of events or people through photographs that tell a story. It can fall under all subjects of photography, but the image needs to be news worthy to end up being published. Remember that the mere taking of pictures is photography not photojournalism.

When selecting pictures for publications you need to remember the following:

  • Technical Value

    • - A picture has a technical value when it’s clear, free from smudges, clear and not blurred.

  • Editorial Value

    • - It has editorial Value when it tells a story at a glance, when it shows life happening, moment of truth and significance, meaning it has news value.

• Five Main Functions of Photos/Illustrations

  • To attract attention
  • To illustrate a point in the story
  • To tell a story itself through the aid of captions
  • To tell a story in sequence with other illustrations
  • To give visual relief to the layout

• Characteristic of a Good Photojournalist

  • ✓ Should have a working knowledge of his outfit - camera, lens, and film.
  • ✓ Should know a little of art and be possessed with a notion of contrast, composition, angle and shape.
  • ✓ Must have a nose for drama, oddity, rarity, action and for human interest stories.
  • ✓ Must be acquainted with the important as well as with the notorious people who break into the news often.
  • ✓ Must have diplomacy and tact when covering risky assignments like fires, riots, rallies, and demonstrations.
  • ✓ Must have acquainted with libel laws, since libel suits can also proceed from pictures.

• Qualities of a Good Photo

  • ✓ Prominence – a pictures of prominent personalities attract to the interest of readers.
  • ✓ Action – “action speaks louder than words”
  • ✓ Human interest – pictures that show emotion
  • ✓ Drama – another form of human interest.
